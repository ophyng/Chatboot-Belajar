ENGLISH LEARNING COMPREHENSIVE GUIDE
======================================
Grammar Rules, TOEFL/IELTS Tips, Writing & Speaking Guide
Compiled for English Language Learners


═══════════════════════════════════════
PART 1: CORE GRAMMAR RULES
═══════════════════════════════════════

1. SUBJECT-VERB AGREEMENT
--------------------------
The subject and verb in a sentence must agree in number (singular or plural).

Rules:
- Singular subject → singular verb (add -s or -es)
- Plural subject → plural verb (no -s)

Examples:
✅ She runs every morning.
✅ They run every morning.
❌ She run every morning. (wrong)
❌ They runs every morning. (wrong)

Special cases:
- "Everyone, anyone, someone, nobody" → singular verb
  ✅ Everyone is welcome here.
- Collective nouns (team, family, group) → usually singular
  ✅ The team is practicing now.
- "Neither...nor / Either...or" → verb agrees with the nearest subject
  ✅ Neither the students nor the teacher was late.


2. VERB TENSES
--------------------------
English has 12 main tenses. The most commonly used:

SIMPLE PRESENT — habits, facts, general truths
Formula: Subject + V1 (+ s/es for he/she/it)
✅ I study English every day.
✅ She works at a hospital.

SIMPLE PAST — completed actions in the past
Formula: Subject + V2
✅ I studied last night.
✅ She worked yesterday.

PRESENT CONTINUOUS — actions happening now or temporary situations
Formula: Subject + is/am/are + V-ing
✅ I am studying right now.
✅ She is working on a project this week.

PRESENT PERFECT — past actions with present relevance
Formula: Subject + has/have + V3
✅ I have studied English for 3 years.
✅ She has worked here since 2020.

PAST CONTINUOUS — actions in progress at a specific past time
Formula: Subject + was/were + V-ing
✅ I was studying when she called.

FUTURE SIMPLE — predictions, decisions, promises
Formula: Subject + will + V1
✅ I will study harder next semester.


3. ARTICLES (A, AN, THE)
--------------------------
A — used before singular countable nouns starting with a consonant sound
✅ a book, a university (note: 'u' sounds like 'yu' = consonant sound)

AN — used before singular countable nouns starting with a vowel sound
✅ an apple, an hour (note: 'h' is silent = vowel sound)

THE — used for specific nouns, things mentioned before, or unique things
✅ The sun rises in the east.
✅ I saw a dog. The dog was brown.

NO ARTICLE — used with plural nouns in general statements, uncountable nouns
✅ Dogs are loyal animals.
✅ Water is essential for life.


4. PREPOSITIONS OF TIME
--------------------------
IN — months, years, seasons, parts of day
✅ in January, in 2024, in summer, in the morning

ON — specific days, dates
✅ on Monday, on March 9th, on Christmas Day

AT — specific times, holiday periods
✅ at 7 o'clock, at noon, at night, at Christmas


5. PREPOSITIONS OF PLACE
--------------------------
IN — inside an area, city, country
✅ in the box, in Jakarta, in Indonesia

ON — on a surface, on a street
✅ on the table, on Sudirman Street

AT — specific point/location
✅ at the door, at the station, at school


6. CONDITIONALS
--------------------------
TYPE 0 — General truths
If + present simple, present simple
✅ If you heat water to 100°C, it boils.

TYPE 1 — Real/possible future situations
If + present simple, will + base verb
✅ If it rains tomorrow, I will stay home.

TYPE 2 — Unreal/hypothetical present situations
If + past simple, would + base verb
✅ If I had more time, I would study abroad.

TYPE 3 — Unreal past situations
If + past perfect, would have + past participle
✅ If I had studied harder, I would have passed.


7. PASSIVE VOICE
--------------------------
Active: Subject + verb + object
✅ The teacher corrects the homework.

Passive: Object + to be + past participle (+ by + agent)
✅ The homework is corrected by the teacher.

Use passive when:
- The doer is unknown or unimportant
- You want to emphasize the action/result

PART 2: TOEFL TIPS & STRATEGIES
═══════════════════════════════════════

TOEFL iBT OVERVIEW
------------------
TOEFL iBT tests 4 skills: Reading, Listening, Speaking, Writing
Total score: 0-120 (each section: 0-30)
Duration: About 3 hours

Common target scores:
- Undergraduate: 61-80
- Graduate: 80-100
- Top universities: 100+

READING SECTION TIPS
--------------------
1. Read the passage quickly first to get the main idea
2. Pay attention to topic sentences (usually first sentence of each paragraph)
3. For vocabulary questions — use context clues, not memorized definitions
4. For "main idea" questions — avoid answers that are too specific or too broad
5. For inference questions — the answer must be logically supported by the text
6. Time management: about 18 minutes per passage

Common question types:
- Factual information: Find specific information stated in the text
- Vocabulary in context: What does the word mean in this context?
- Inference: What can be concluded from the passage?
- Main idea: What is the passage mainly about?
- Sentence insertion: Where does this sentence best fit?

LISTENING SECTION TIPS
----------------------
1. Take notes while listening — especially names, numbers, key terms
2. Focus on main ideas, not every detail
3. Pay attention to speaker's tone and attitude
4. For conversations: what is the problem and what is the solution?
5. For lectures: what is the main topic and what are the supporting points?

SPEAKING SECTION TIPS
---------------------
Task 1 (Independent): Give your opinion on a familiar topic
- State your opinion clearly in the first sentence
- Give 2 supporting reasons with examples
- Speak clearly and at a natural pace
- Template: "I believe/prefer... because... First... Second... Therefore..."

Tasks 2-4 (Integrated): Reading + Listening + Speaking
- Summarize what you read AND heard
- Show connection between the two sources
- Use transitions: "According to the reading... However, the professor argues..."

WRITING SECTION TIPS
--------------------
Task 1 (Integrated): Reading + Listening → 150-225 words
- Summarize the lecture and how it relates to the reading
- The lecture usually CHALLENGES the reading
- Use phrases: "The professor argues that... This contradicts the reading which states..."

Task 2 (Independent): Essay on a given topic → 300+ words
Essay structure:
- Introduction: Hook + background + thesis statement
- Body paragraph 1: Point 1 + explanation + example
- Body paragraph 2: Point 2 + explanation + example
- Conclusion: Restate thesis + summary + closing thought

Useful transitions:
- Adding: Furthermore, Moreover, In addition, Additionally
- Contrasting: However, Nevertheless, On the other hand, Although
- Giving examples: For instance, For example, Such as, To illustrate
- Concluding: In conclusion, Therefore, Thus, As a result

PART 3: IELTS TIPS & STRATEGIES
═══════════════════════════════════════

IELTS OVERVIEW
--------------
IELTS tests 4 skills: Listening, Reading, Writing, Speaking
Band score: 0-9 (each section)
Two types: Academic (university) and General Training (work/migration)

Common target band scores:
- University admission: 6.0-7.0
- Professional registration: 7.0+
- Top universities: 7.5-8.0

READING TIPS (Academic)
-----------------------
1. Skim the passage first — read title, headings, first and last sentences
2. Read questions BEFORE reading the passage in detail
3. For True/False/Not Given:
   - TRUE: information matches the passage
   - FALSE: information contradicts the passage
   - NOT GIVEN: information not mentioned at all
4. For matching headings: identify the main idea of each paragraph
5. Answers appear in order in the passage (except matching tasks)

LISTENING TIPS
--------------
1. Read the questions during the preparation time
2. Answers come in the order you hear them
3. Spell names and places carefully
4. Don't leave blanks — make an educated guess
5. Check grammar: if the answer is a verb, make sure it fits grammatically

WRITING TASK 1 (Academic) — Describe a graph/chart/diagram
- Overview is ESSENTIAL — write 2-3 sentences about the main trend
- Compare and contrast the most significant features
- Use data to support your description
- Do NOT give opinions or conclusions
- Useful language: "rose sharply to...", "remained stable at...", "accounted for..."

WRITING TASK 2 — Essay (250+ words)
Same structure as TOEFL Task 2:
Introduction → Body 1 → Body 2 → Conclusion

Common essay types:
- Opinion/Argumentative: "Do you agree or disagree?"
- Discussion: "Discuss both views and give your opinion"
- Problem-solution: "What are the problems? What are the solutions?"
- Advantages-disadvantages: "Discuss the advantages and disadvantages"

SPEAKING TIPS
-------------
Part 1 (Introduction): 4-5 minutes — questions about yourself, home, work, interests
- Give extended answers — don't just say yes/no
- Use a variety of vocabulary and tenses

Part 2 (Long turn): 2 minutes — speak about a topic card
- Use the 1 minute preparation time to make notes
- Cover all the points on the card
- Keep talking for the full 2 minutes

Part 3 (Discussion): 4-5 minutes — abstract questions related to Part 2 topic
- Give well-developed answers
- Show ability to discuss ideas, speculate, compare

PART 4: WRITING SKILLS
═══════════════════════════════════════

PARAGRAPH STRUCTURE
-------------------
Every good paragraph has 3 parts:
1. Topic sentence — states the main idea
2. Supporting sentences — explain, give evidence, examples
3. Concluding sentence — wraps up the paragraph

Example:
"Regular exercise has numerous benefits for mental health. [TOPIC]
Studies show that physical activity releases endorphins, which improve mood and reduce anxiety. For instance, people who exercise at least 30 minutes a day report lower levels of stress compared to those who are sedentary. [SUPPORT]
Therefore, incorporating exercise into daily routines is an effective way to maintain emotional wellbeing. [CONCLUSION]"

ESSAY STRUCTURE
---------------
Introduction (10-15% of essay):
- Hook: interesting fact, question, or statement
- Background: context for the topic
- Thesis: your main argument/position

Body (70-80% of essay):
- Each paragraph = one main idea
- Start with topic sentence
- Support with evidence and examples
- Use transitions between paragraphs

Conclusion (10-15% of essay):
- Restate thesis (in different words)
- Summarize main points
- Closing thought/call to action/broader implication

USEFUL TRANSITIONS
------------------
To add information:
Furthermore, Moreover, In addition, Additionally, Also, Besides

To show contrast:
However, Nevertheless, On the other hand, Although, Despite, In contrast

To show cause/effect:
Therefore, Thus, As a result, Consequently, Due to, Because of

To give examples:
For example, For instance, Such as, To illustrate, Namely

To conclude:
In conclusion, To summarize, Overall, In summary, Finally

PART 5: SPEAKING SKILLS
═══════════════════════════════════════

COMMON SPEAKING MISTAKES TO AVOID
-----------------------------------
1. Translating directly from Indonesian to English
   ❌ "I go to school by foot" → ✅ "I walk to school"
   ❌ "My age is 20 years" → ✅ "I am 20 years old"

2. Wrong use of "since" and "for"
   ❌ "I have studied English since 3 years"
   ✅ "I have studied English for 3 years"
   ✅ "I have studied English since 2021"

3. Forgetting to add -s for third person singular
   ❌ "She go to work every day"
   ✅ "She goes to work every day"

USEFUL SPEAKING PHRASES
-----------------------
Agreeing:
- I completely agree with that.
- That's a good point.
- Absolutely! / Exactly!
- I couldn't agree more.

Disagreeing (politely):
- I see your point, but...
- I'm not sure I agree because...
- That's interesting, however...
- I respectfully disagree.

Asking for clarification:
- Could you elaborate on that?
- What do you mean by...?
- Could you give an example?
- I'm not sure I understand. Could you repeat that?

Buying time while thinking:
- That's an interesting question...
- Let me think about that for a moment...
- Well, as far as I know...
- That's a good question. I think...

PRONUNCIATION TIPS
------------------
Common mispronunciations by Indonesian speakers:
- "Comfortable" → say "KUMF-ter-bul" (3 syllables, not 4)
- "Vegetable" → say "VEJ-tuh-bul" (3 syllables)
- "Wednesday" → say "WENZ-day" (the 'd' is silent)
- "Clothes" → say "KLOHZ" (the 'th' sounds like 'z')
- "Months" → say "MUNTHS" (don't forget the 'ths' at the end)
- "Colleagues" → say "KOL-eegz"
- "Especially" → say "es-PESH-uh-lee" (not "eks-pesh-uh-lee")
- "Specific" → say "spuh-SIF-ik" (not "eks-peh-SIF-ik")

PART 6: COMMON ENGLISH MISTAKES BY
         INDONESIAN SPEAKERS
═══════════════════════════════════════

1. DOUBLE SUBJECT
❌ My mother she is a teacher.
✅ My mother is a teacher.

2. WRONG WORD ORDER
❌ I have problem a.
✅ I have a problem.

3. MISSING ARTICLE
❌ She is student.
✅ She is a student.

4. WRONG PRONOUN
❌ My friend, he name is Budi.
✅ My friend's name is Budi.

5. OVERUSING "VERY"
❌ It is very very beautiful.
✅ It is extremely beautiful. / It is gorgeous.

6. "DISCUSS ABOUT"
❌ Let's discuss about the topic.
✅ Let's discuss the topic. (no "about" needed)

7. "EXPLAIN ABOUT"
❌ Can you explain about this?
✅ Can you explain this?

8. "MENTION ABOUT"
❌ She mentioned about the problem.
✅ She mentioned the problem.

9. WRONG USE OF "BORING" vs "BORED"
❌ I am boring. (means you are a boring person!)
✅ I am bored. (you feel bored)
✅ The class is boring. (the class causes boredom)

10. WRONG USE OF "INTERESTING" vs "INTERESTED"
❌ I am interesting in English.
✅ I am interested in English.
✅ English is interesting.
