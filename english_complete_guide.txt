1.5 TIME (WAKTU)

PM = siang/malam hari (12:00 - 23:59)

Parts of the day:
- midnight = tengah malam (12 AM)
- morning = pagi (5 AM - 12 PM)
- noon = tengah hari (12 PM)
- afternoon = sore (12 PM - 6 PM)
- evening = petang/malam awal (6 PM - 9 PM)

Time expressions:
- now = sekarang
- soon = segera
- later = nanti
- ago = yang lalu (e.g., 2 years ago = 2 tahun yang lalu)
- in = dalam waktu (e.g., in 5 minutes = dalam 5 menit)

2.1 NOUNS (KATA BENDA)
-----------------------
Noun = kata benda (orang, tempat, benda, konsep)

Types of nouns:
a) Common noun = kata benda umum: book, city, dog
b) Proper noun = nama khusus (huruf kapital): Jakarta, John, Monday
c) Countable noun = bisa dihitung: apple → apples
d) Uncountable noun = tidak bisa dihitung: water, rice, information
e) Abstract noun = konsep/perasaan: love, freedom, happiness
f) Collective noun = kelompok: team, family, class, flock

Plural rules:
- Most nouns: add -s → book/books, cat/cats
- Ends in -s, -ss, -sh, -ch, -x: add -es → bus/buses, watch/watches
- Ends in consonant + y: change y to i, add -es → city/cities
- Ends in -f or -fe: change to -ves → leaf/leaves, knife/knives
- Irregular plurals:
  man/men, woman/women, child/children, tooth/teeth,
  foot/feet, mouse/mice, person/people, fish/fish,
  sheep/sheep, deer/deer, ox/oxen


2.2 PRONOUNS (KATA GANTI)
---------------------------
Personal pronouns:
Subject    Object     Possessive Adj   Possessive Pron   Reflexive
I          me         my               mine              myself
you        you        your             yours             yourself
he         him        his              his               himself
she        her        her              hers              herself
it         it         its              its               itself
we         us         our              ours              ourselves
they       them       their            theirs            themselves

Examples:
- I love my job. The job is mine.
- She gave him her book. The book is hers.
- They did it themselves.

Demonstrative pronouns:
- this = ini (singular, dekat)
- that = itu (singular, jauh)
- these = ini (plural, dekat)
- those = itu (plural, jauh)

Interrogative pronouns:
- who = siapa (orang)
- whom = siapa (objek)
- whose = milik siapa
- which = yang mana
- what = apa


2.3 ADJECTIVES (KATA SIFAT)
-----------------------------
Adjective = kata yang menerangkan noun

Order of adjectives (urutan kata sifat):
Opinion → Size → Age → Shape → Color → Origin → Material → Purpose + Noun
Example: a beautiful (opinion) small (size) old (age) round (shape) 
         red (color) Italian (origin) silver (material) dining (purpose) table

Comparative adjectives (perbandingan):
- Short adj (1-2 syllable): add -er + than
  big → bigger than, small → smaller than, fast → faster than
- Long adj (3+ syllables): more/less + adj + than
  beautiful → more beautiful than, expensive → more expensive than
- Irregular:
  good → better than         bad → worse than
  far → farther/further than many/much → more than
  little → less than

Superlative adjectives (paling):
- Short adj: the + adj + -est
  big → the biggest, tall → the tallest
- Long adj: the most/least + adj
  beautiful → the most beautiful
- Irregular:
  good → the best            bad → the worst
  far → the farthest/furthest many/much → the most

2.5 ADVERBS (KATA KETERANGAN)
------------------------------
Adverb = menerangkan verb, adjective, atau adverb lain

Types:
a) Manner (cara): slowly, quickly, carefully, well, hard
b) Time (waktu): now, soon, yesterday, already, still, yet
c) Frequency (frekuensi): always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never
d) Place (tempat): here, there, everywhere, somewhere
e) Degree (tingkat): very, quite, rather, too, enough, extremely

Frequency adverbs (urutan dari paling sering):
100% always → usually → often → sometimes → rarely → seldom → never 0%

Position:
- Before main verb: She always studies hard.
- After 'be': He is never late.
- At start/end for emphasis: Sometimes I eat late.


2.6 PREPOSITIONS (KATA DEPAN)
-------------------------------
Time prepositions:
- at: specific time → at 7 PM, at noon, at night
- on: days/dates → on Monday, on March 9th
- in: months/years/seasons/periods → in January, in 2024, in summer, in the morning
- for: duration → for 3 hours, for two years
- since: starting point → since 2020, since Monday
- during: throughout a period → during the holiday
- by: deadline → by Friday, by 5 PM
- until/till: up to a point → until midnight
- from...to: range → from Monday to Friday
- before: earlier than → before noon
- after: later than → after dinner

Place prepositions:
- at: specific point → at the door, at school, at home
- in: inside → in the box, in Jakarta, in the room
- on: surface → on the table, on the wall, on the floor
- above: higher than, no contact → above the clouds
- over: higher than, covering → a bridge over the river
- below: lower than → below sea level
- under: directly below → under the table
- beside/next to: at the side → beside the window
- between: in the middle of two → between A and B
- among: in the middle of many → among the crowd
- in front of: facing → in front of the school
- behind: at the back → behind the building
- near/close to: not far → near the station
- far from: distant → far from home
- opposite: facing, across → opposite the bank
- around: surrounding → around the corner
- along: following the length → along the road
- across: from one side to other → across the street
- through: from one end to another → through the tunnel
- into: movement to inside → walk into the room
- out of: movement from inside → get out of the car
- onto: movement to a surface → jump onto the table
- off: away from → fall off the bike
- up: to a higher position → walk up the stairs
- down: to a lower position → run down the hill
- towards: in the direction of → walk towards the exit

BAGIAN 3: TENSES LENGKAP
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

3.1 SIMPLE PRESENT
-------------------
Penggunaan:
- Kebiasaan/rutinitas: I brush my teeth every morning.
- Fakta umum/kebenaran: The earth revolves around the sun.
- Jadwal tetap: The train leaves at 8 AM.
- Perasaan/opini saat ini: I love chocolate.

Formula:
(+) S + V1(+s/es) → She works. / They work.
(-) S + don't/doesn't + V1 → She doesn't work. / They don't work.
(?) Do/Does + S + V1? → Does she work? / Do they work?

Third person singular rules (-s/-es):
- Most verbs: add -s → work/works, eat/eats
- Ends in -s, -sh, -ch, -x, -o: add -es → go/goes, watch/watches
- Ends in consonant + y: y → ies → study/studies, fly/flies


3.2 PRESENT CONTINUOUS
------------------------
Penggunaan:
- Sedang terjadi sekarang: I am eating now.
- Situasi sementara: She is staying at a hotel this week.
- Rencana pasti di masa depan: We are meeting tomorrow.
- Perubahan/tren: Prices are rising.

Formula:
(+) S + is/am/are + V-ing → She is studying.
(-) S + is/am/are + not + V-ing → She is not studying.
(?) Is/Am/Are + S + V-ing? → Is she studying?

V-ing spelling rules:
- Most verbs: add -ing → eat/eating, work/working
- Ends in -e: drop e, add -ing → write/writing, make/making
- Short vowel + consonant: double consonant → run/running, sit/sitting

Non-continuous verbs (tidak bisa pakai -ing):
want, need, like, love, hate, prefer, know, understand,
believe, think (opini), have (kepemilikan), see, hear, smell


3.3 SIMPLE PAST
----------------
Penggunaan:
- Kejadian selesai di masa lalu: I visited Bali last year.
- Urutan kejadian di masa lalu: She woke up, showered, and had breakfast.

Formula:
(+) S + V2 → She studied. / They went.
(-) S + didn't + V1 → She didn't study. / They didn't go.
(?) Did + S + V1? → Did she study? / Did they go?

Regular past tense (-ed):
- Most verbs: add -ed → work/worked, play/played
- Ends in -e: add -d → live/lived, love/loved
- Ends in consonant + y: y → ied → study/studied, carry/carried
- Short vowel + consonant: double → stop/stopped, plan/planned


3.4 PAST CONTINUOUS
--------------------
Penggunaan:
- Sedang berlangsung di waktu tertentu di masa lalu: I was sleeping at 10 PM.
- Dua aksi bersamaan di masa lalu: While I was cooking, she was reading.
- Aksi yang terinterupsi: I was studying when she called.

Formula:
(+) S + was/were + V-ing → She was working.
(-) S + was/were + not + V-ing → She wasn't working.
(?) Was/Were + S + V-ing? → Was she working?


3.5 PRESENT PERFECT
--------------------
Penggunaan:
- Pengalaman hidup (ever/never): Have you ever been to Paris?
- Aksi baru saja selesai (just): I have just finished my homework.
- Aksi berlanjut dari masa lalu hingga sekarang (for/since): I have lived here for 5 years.
- Aksi yang hasilnya masih relevan: She has lost her keys. (masih hilang)

Formula:
(+) S + have/has + V3 → She has eaten. / They have eaten.
(-) S + have/has + not + V3 → She hasn't eaten. / They haven't eaten.
(?) Have/Has + S + V3? → Has she eaten? / Have they eaten?

Key words: already, yet, just, ever, never, for, since, recently, lately


3.6 PRESENT PERFECT CONTINUOUS
--------------------------------
Penggunaan:
- Aksi yang sudah berlangsung lama dan masih terus berlanjut: I have been studying for 3 hours.
- Untuk menekankan durasi: She has been working here since 2020.

Formula:
(+) S + have/has + been + V-ing → She has been working.
(-) S + have/has + not + been + V-ing → She hasn't been working.
(?) Have/Has + S + been + V-ing? → Has she been working?


3.7 SIMPLE FUTURE
------------------
Will:
- Keputusan spontan: I'll have the pasta, please.
- Prediksi: It will rain tomorrow.
- Janji: I will call you later.
- Tawaran: I'll help you with that.

Be going to:
- Rencana yang sudah diputuskan: I'm going to study abroad next year.
- Prediksi berdasarkan bukti: Look at those clouds — it's going to rain.

Formula will:
(+) S + will + V1 → She will come.
(-) S + will + not (won't) + V1 → She won't come.
(?) Will + S + V1? → Will she come?


3.8 FUTURE CONTINUOUS
----------------------
Penggunaan:
- Aksi yang akan sedang berlangsung di waktu tertentu di masa depan: I will be sleeping at midnight.
- Aksi yang sudah terencana: This time next week, I will be traveling to Bali.

Formula:
(+) S + will + be + V-ing → She will be working tomorrow.


3.9 PAST PERFECT
-----------------
Penggunaan:
- Aksi yang terjadi SEBELUM aksi lain di masa lalu: When I arrived, she had already left.
- Kondisi sebelum kejadian lain: He was tired because he had worked all day.

Formula:
(+) S + had + V3 → She had finished.
(-) S + had + not + V3 → She hadn't finished.
(?) Had + S + V3? → Had she finished?

BAGIAN 4: SENTENCE TYPES (JENIS KALIMAT)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

4.1 BASIC SENTENCE STRUCTURES
-------------------------------
Simple sentence: 1 independent clause
→ She studies English.

Compound sentence: 2 independent clauses + coordinating conjunction (FANBOYS)
For, And, Nor, But, Or, Yet, So
→ She studies English, and she practices every day.

Complex sentence: 1 independent + 1 dependent clause + subordinating conjunction
→ Although she is busy, she still finds time to study.

Compound-complex: multiple clauses
→ Although she is busy, she studies every day, and she is improving fast.


4.2 QUESTION TYPES
-------------------
Yes/No questions:
- Are you a student? → Yes, I am. / No, I'm not.
- Do you like coffee? → Yes, I do. / No, I don't.
- Did she call? → Yes, she did. / No, she didn't.

WH- questions:
- What = apa → What is your name?
- Who = siapa → Who is your teacher?
- Where = di mana → Where do you live?
- When = kapan → When did you arrive?
- Why = kenapa → Why are you late?
- How = bagaimana → How are you?
- Which = yang mana → Which do you prefer?
- Whose = milik siapa → Whose book is this?
- How much = berapa (uncountable) → How much does it cost?
- How many = berapa banyak (countable) → How many students are there?
- How long = berapa lama → How long have you been here?
- How often = seberapa sering → How often do you exercise?
- How far = seberapa jauh → How far is the station?

Tag questions:
- She is a doctor, isn't she?
- They don't like coffee, do they?
- He called yesterday, didn't he?
- You can drive, can't you?


4.3 NEGATIVE SENTENCES
------------------------
With auxiliary verbs: add "not"
- She is not (isn't) happy.
- They have not (haven't) eaten.
- He will not (won't) come.
- I cannot (can't) swim.

With do/does/did:
- I do not (don't) like spicy food.
- She does not (doesn't) work here.
- We did not (didn't) go yesterday.

BAGIAN 5: MODAL VERBS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

5.1 CAN / COULD
----------------
Can = bisa (kemampuan sekarang, izin informal, kemungkinan)
- I can speak English. (kemampuan)
- Can I use your phone? (izin informal)
- It can be dangerous. (kemungkinan)

Could = bisa (kemampuan masa lalu, izin formal, saran, kemungkinan)
- I could swim when I was 5. (kemampuan masa lalu)
- Could I borrow your pen? (izin formal/sopan)
- You could try a different approach. (saran)
- It could rain later. (kemungkinan)


5.2 WILL / WOULD
-----------------
Will = akan (prediksi, keputusan spontan, janji)
- It will be sunny tomorrow.
- I'll have the soup, please.
- I will call you back.

Would = akan/mau (kondisional, permintaan sopan, kebiasaan masa lalu)
- I would travel if I had money. (kondisional)
- Would you please close the door? (sopan)
- When I was young, I would play football every day. (kebiasaan masa lalu)


5.3 MAY / MIGHT
----------------
May = boleh/mungkin (izin formal, kemungkinan 50%)
- May I come in? (izin formal)
- It may rain this afternoon. (kemungkinan)

Might = mungkin (kemungkinan lebih kecil, <50%)
- I might go to the party. (tidak terlalu yakin)
- She might be at home. (tidak pasti)


5.4 MUST / HAVE TO
-------------------
Must = harus (kewajiban internal, kesimpulan logis)
- You must wear a seatbelt. (aturan)
- He must be tired — he worked all day. (kesimpulan)

Have to = harus (kewajiban eksternal/aturan)
- I have to submit the report by Monday. (deadline dari luar)
- She has to wear a uniform. (aturan sekolah/kerja)

Must not (mustn't) = tidak boleh (larangan)
- You mustn't smoke here.

Don't have to = tidak perlu (tidak ada kewajiban)
- You don't have to come if you're busy.


5.5 SHOULD / OUGHT TO
----------------------
Should = seharusnya (saran, rekomendasi, ekspektasi)
- You should see a doctor.
- She should be here by now.

Ought to = seharusnya (mirip should, sedikit lebih formal)
- You ought to apologize.
- He ought to know better.

Should have = seharusnya sudah (penyesalan tentang masa lalu)
- I should have studied harder. (tapi tidak)
- She shouldn't have said that. (tapi sudah terlanjur)

BAGIAN 6: CONJUNCTIONS (KATA HUBUNG)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

6.1 COORDINATING CONJUNCTIONS (FANBOYS)
-----------------------------------------
For = karena (alasan) → She left early, for she was tired.
And = dan → I like coffee and tea.
Nor = dan juga tidak → He doesn't smoke, nor does he drink.
But = tapi → I'm tired, but I have to finish.
Or = atau → Do you want tea or coffee?
Yet = namun/tetapi → It's expensive, yet it's worth it.
So = jadi/sehingga → It was raining, so we stayed home.


6.2 SUBORDINATING CONJUNCTIONS
--------------------------------
Time:
- when = ketika → Call me when you arrive.
- while = sementara → She sang while cooking.
- before = sebelum → Brush your teeth before going to bed.
- after = setelah → I'll call you after I finish.
- as soon as = segera setelah → Call me as soon as you land.
- until = sampai → Wait here until I come back.
- since = sejak → I haven't seen her since graduation.
- once = begitu/setelah → Once you start, don't stop.

Cause:
- because = karena → I'm tired because I worked late.
- since = karena → Since you're here, let's start.
- as = karena → As it was raining, we stayed inside.
- due to = karena (+ noun) → Due to the rain, the match was cancelled.

Contrast:
- although/though/even though = meskipun → Although it rained, we went out.
- while = sementara di sisi lain → While I like jazz, she prefers pop.
- whereas = sedangkan → He is tall, whereas his brother is short.
- despite/in spite of = meskipun (+ noun) → Despite the rain, we went out.

Condition:
- if = jika → If it rains, I'll stay home.
- unless = kecuali jika → I'll go unless it rains.
- provided that = asalkan → I'll help, provided that you help me too.
- as long as = selama/asalkan → You can stay as long as you want.

Purpose:
- so that = agar/supaya → I study hard so that I can pass.
- in order to = untuk → She works hard in order to succeed.

Result:
- so...that = sangat...sehingga → It was so hot that we stayed inside.
- such...that = begitu...sehingga → It was such a hot day that we stayed inside.


6.3 CORRELATIVE CONJUNCTIONS
------------------------------
both...and = keduanya → Both she and he are smart.
either...or = salah satu → Either you come or I'll go.
neither...nor = tidak keduanya → Neither she nor he was there.
not only...but also = tidak hanya...tetapi juga → She is not only smart but also hardworking.
whether...or = apakah...atau → I don't know whether to stay or leave.

BAGIAN 7: DIRECT & INDIRECT SPEECH
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Direct speech = kutipan langsung
→ She said, "I am tired."

Indirect speech = laporan tidak langsung
→ She said (that) she was tired.

Tense changes in indirect speech:
Direct                    Indirect
present simple      →     past simple
present continuous  →     past continuous
present perfect     →     past perfect
past simple         →     past perfect
will                →     would
can                 →     could
may                 →     might
must                →     had to

Pronoun and time changes:
I → he/she              now → then
you → I/he/she          today → that day
here → there            tomorrow → the next day
this → that             yesterday → the day before
these → those           last week → the week before
tonight → that night    next week → the following week

Examples:
Direct: "I will call you tomorrow," she said.
Indirect: She said that she would call me the next day.

Direct: "Are you coming?" he asked.
Indirect: He asked if/whether I was coming.

Direct: "Please sit down," the teacher said.
Indirect: The teacher told us to sit down.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
BAGIAN 8: PASSIVE VOICE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Active: S + verb + object
→ The chef cooks the food.

Passive: Object + to be + past participle (+ by + agent)
→ The food is cooked by the chef.

Passive forms for all tenses:
Tense           Active                    Passive
Simple Present  She writes the report.    The report is written.
Present Cont.   She is writing it.        It is being written.
Simple Past     She wrote the report.     The report was written.
Past Cont.      She was writing it.       It was being written.
Present Perf.   She has written it.       It has been written.
Past Perfect    She had written it.       It had been written.
Simple Future   She will write it.        It will be written.
Modal           She can write it.         It can be written.

When to use passive:
- Doer unknown: The window was broken. (don't know who)
- Doer unimportant: The report was submitted on time.
- To emphasize the action/result: The contract was signed yesterday.
- Formal/scientific writing: The experiment was conducted...
